My Story

My name is Nick Brooks, and I have certainly taken a circuitous route to get to this point! I began my career pursuing filmmaking, earning a degree in Digital Filmmaking and Video Production from the University of Central Arkansas. I was pursuing a Master of Fine Arts from the University of Texas - Austin when the COVID-19 Pandemic shut down our film productions. I took this time to reevaluate my career path, and came to the conclusion to pursue a career as a data analyst. I have always been a sports fan, and one of my favorite pastimes is looking over statistics of various players and teams in an attempt to figure out why certain teams were successful and others were not. As such, I knew that a career in Data Science was the path for me!



I have earned a Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science from Oregon State University, graduating Magna Cum Laude. I am wrapping up my last semester at Willamette University, where I will earn a Master of Science in Data Science. With my well-rounded and diverse skill-set and my positive, friendly attitude, I am confident that I would be a strong addition to any data analysis team. For more information on my projects and my technologies of choice, please explore my portfolio site!

MLB Ejections Analysis

   

I analyzed historical MLB ejections data to find key trends in ejections, such as when are players most likely to be ejected and what reasons most often lead to ejections. Along with my project partner Jonathan, we created eye catching data visualizations to help present our findings to the public, and to help inform MLB managers of situations that can lead to ejections.
